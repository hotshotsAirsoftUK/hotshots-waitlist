Privacy Policy

Effective date: 17 May 2026  ·  Hotshots Airsoft Ltd  ·  Company No. 17158689

Plain English Summary

Hotshots Airsoft Ltd is committed to protecting your privacy. We collect your personal information only where necessary — to manage waitlist sign-ups, conduct market research, and (when we open for bookings) to process reservations and ensure participant safety. We do not sell your data or use it for marketing without your explicit consent.

Hotshots Airsoft Ltd is the data controller for all personal data you provide through our website or at our events.

2. Data We Collect

Waitlist sign-up Active

When you join our pre-launch waitlist, we collect your first name, last name, email address, and optional details including company name, job title, approximate group size, event type, and how you heard about us. This is used solely to notify you when bookings open.

Market research survey Active

If you complete our market research survey, we collect your survey responses to inform internal business planning. Your email address is only collected if you opt in to receive launch updates at the end of the survey.

Bookings and event participation Pre-launch

When we open for bookings, we will collect your name, email address, telephone number, and home address to confirm and manage your reservation. We do not collect payment card details directly — payments will be processed by a third-party payment provider (details confirmed prior to launch).

All participants are required to complete and sign a Participant Waiver and Release of Liability before taking part in any activity. This document records your name, date of birth, email address, telephone number, and signature. It also includes a medical screening questionnaire covering conditions that may affect safe participation, including eye health, hearing, cardiovascular conditions, and pregnancy status. This constitutes special category personal data under UK GDPR Article 9 (health data). We collect it under Article 9(2)(a) — your explicit consent, given freely at the point of signing, for the purpose of ensuring your safety and the safety of other participants.

For site-based events, the person or organisation providing the venue is required to sign a separate Customer Site Liability Agreement prior to the session. This records the site owner's name, contact number, site address, and declared hazards. This is a commercial record and is processed under our legitimate interests in documenting site conditions for safety and insurance purposes.

3. How We Use Your Data

  • To notify waitlist members when bookings open.
  • To conduct anonymised internal analysis of market research responses.
  • To process and manage event bookings and communicate with you about your reservation.
  • To ensure compliance with safety regulations and maintain a safe environment for all participants.
  • To fulfil our legal and insurance obligations in relation to participant waivers.

We will not use your personal data for marketing purposes unless you have explicitly consented. You may withdraw that consent at any time.

4. Data Storage and Security

All digital data collected through our website is stored securely by our third-party form processors (see Section 6). We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your data from unauthorised access, alteration, loss, or disclosure.

Participant liability waivers signed at events will be stored in a locked file, accessible only by authorised personnel. We apply the same standard of care to physical records as to digital ones.

5. Data Retention

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purpose for which it was collected:

  • Waitlist data: held until you request deletion, withdraw consent, or 12 months after the launch notification has been sent with no further engagement from you.
  • Market research responses: anonymised aggregates are retained for business planning; any personally identifiable responses are deleted within 12 months of collection.
  • Booking data: retained for the duration required by our accounting, insurance, and legal obligations, then securely deleted.
  • Participant waivers and medical screening data: retained for a minimum of 6 years, as required by our public liability insurance provider and applicable law. We will confirm the retention period to each participant prior to their first session.
  • Customer Site Liability Agreements: retained for a minimum of 6 years from the date of the session, in line with the Limitation Act 1980 for contractual claims.

If Hotshots Airsoft does not launch within 24 months of your waitlist sign-up, we will delete all waitlist data and notify you by email before doing so.

7. Your Rights

Under UK GDPR you have the following rights in relation to your personal data. To exercise any of them, email info@hotshotsairsoft.co.uk. We will respond within one calendar month and may ask you to verify your identity first.

Right to Access
Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
Right to Rectification
Request corrections to any inaccurate or incomplete data we hold.
Right to Erasure
Request deletion of your personal data. We will action this within 30 days, subject to any legal retention obligations (e.g. liability waivers required by our insurer).
Right to Withdraw Consent
Withdraw your consent to marketing or data processing at any time. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.
Right to Restriction
Request that we limit how we use your data in certain circumstances.
Right to Data Portability
Request your data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format.
Right to Object
Object to processing based on legitimate interests. Note: our current processing is based on consent, so this right does not currently apply.
